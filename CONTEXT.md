# Context Glossary

Shared language for the productized-social-content venture aimed at coffee roasteries.
Glossary only - no implementation detail.

## Kaffeerösterei (Coffee Roastery)

Target customer. Small business roasting/selling coffee. Revenue mix leans on
Laufkundschaft and third-party platforms. Owner-operated, time-constrained.

## Laufkundschaft (Walk-in Trade)

Foot-traffic revenue. Unpredictable, geographically capped, not owned by the
roastery. The dependence the venture aims to reduce.

## Online-Umsatz (Online Revenue)

Revenue through the roastery's own channel (Shopify store). Higher margin than
third-party platforms because no platform cut is given away. The growth target.

## Online-Kanal (Owned Channel)

The roastery's own storefront + social presence. "Bespielen" = consistently
feeding it content. Higher margin, but costs owner time.

## Productized Service

The offering: high-volume, done-for-you social-media content sold as a repeatable
package. Marginal cost per post drives toward zero (vs. an agency's per-post labor
price), so the roastery can afford the consistency that actually moves revenue.
Owner load near zero: approve/schedule, or fully automated. Analytics-driven -
content is steered by what performed (Reels vs. image posts vs. story).

## Consistency Wedge

Central insight. The agency priced per post (labor model), so the roastery could
only afford low volume; low volume = no consistency = no results. Consistency is
the single factor that moved the owner's Online-Umsatz. Productization breaks the
labor-cost link, making affordable consistency possible. This is the
differentiator over [Marketingagentur].

## Marketingagentur (Agency) - the competitor

Prior solution the owner tried. Per-post pricing, full-channel takeover only at
mid four-figure/month. Too expensive, too little volume booked, no results. The
"800-pound gorilla" slot in the founding hypothesis.

## Online-Marge (Online Margin)

Profit left on an online order after COGS. Pressured by coffee bean price spikes
that already forced the owner to drop some SKUs as uneconomical. A precondition:
if margin is too thin, more Online-Umsatz is not worth chasing.

## Capacity (Supply Precondition)

Whether the roastery can roast/fulfill more volume if online demand rises. If
growth breaks fulfillment, demand generation is premature.

## Validation Gates (Friday, kill-early order)

1. Desire - does the owner want (passive) Online-Umsatz growth at all?
2. Economics - is Online-Marge sufficient to make growth worth it?
3. Capacity - can supply scale without breaking?
4. Content pain - the consistency wedge. Only matters if 1-3 hold.

## Founding Hypothesis (working)

If we help a [Kaffeerösterei] sustain consistent, analytics-steered social content
[approach: productized, near-zero marginal cost, near-zero owner load], they choose
it over a [Marketingagentur] because they get the volume/consistency that drives
Online-Umsatz at a price Laufkundschaft can fund.

## Pilot

First engagement: one month of content for the existing customer, currently
framed as free, used to learn delivery and validate demand.

## Pain Point (working)

Owner knows social content drives Online-Umsatz (observed it himself) but cannot
sustain it - "keine Zeit". Prior agency engagement did not stick (reason TBD).
